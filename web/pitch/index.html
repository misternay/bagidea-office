The problem

AI agents are powerful.
And completely invisible.

They live trapped in a chat window or a scrolling terminal. You can't see a team working, can't tell who's doing what, and close the tab and the "office" is gone. Multi-agent work feels like managing ghosts you can't point at.

no presence

A wall of text

One chat thread can't show five agents collaborating. Parallel work collapses into a transcript nobody can follow.

no place

Nowhere to live

Close the window and it's over. Your "AI team" has no home, no continuity, no sense of an ongoing workplace.

no control

Trust, blindly

Agents touch your files and run commands off-screen. Approving actions means reading log lines, not watching the room.

The solution

Give the team a place to work.

BagIdea Office turns your wallpaper into a living HD-2D office. Every character is a real Claude Code agent — give an order to the Director and watch him walk it to the right desk, split the work, and report back. Behind the pixels it's real engineering in real folders.

Why it's different

Things only an office can do.

parallelism

Ghost clones

An agent splits into translucent clones that work in parallel on a Ghost Deck, then merges — visible the whole time.

safety

Spatial permissions

A risky action raises an approval card over the agent's head. Allow per-tool, once or forever.

delegation

A real Director

Talk to one manager. He plans, assigns to whoever fits, and walks the result back to your desk.

reach

Hands on the web

One skill and an agent drives a real browser — open pages, click, fill forms — watch it or run it quietly.

memory

Learns on the job

Agents pick up skills and keep notes across sessions — the office sharpens with use.

extensible

Agents build tools

The office writes its own plugins — new panels and commands — then installs them on itself.

Swappable brains

Hire each one a different mind.

Per agent, pick the model that fits the job and the budget — frontier Claude for the hard calls, fast cheap models for grunt work, or a fully local brain for zero cloud. One office, many minds, no lock-in.
